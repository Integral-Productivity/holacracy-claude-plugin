# Constitutional Reference -- Secretary Role (Holacracy v5.0)

This file contains Secretary-relevant provisions from the Holacracy Constitution v5.0. Load it when drafting constitutional interpretations, advising on process validity, or when a detailed constitutional basis is needed.

---

## Secretary Role Definition (Appendix A)

**Purpose:** Stabilize the Circle's constitutionally-required records and meetings

**Accountabilities:**
- Scheduling the Circle's constitutionally-required meetings
- Capturing and publishing the outputs of the Circle's Governance Process
- Capturing and publishing Tactical Meeting outputs
- Interpreting the Constitution and anything under its authority upon request

**Domain:** The Circle's governance records

---

## Article 3: Tactical Process

### S.3.1 -- Tactical Meetings
The Secretary of each Circle is accountable for scheduling regular Tactical Meetings for the Circle. All of the Circle's Roles are invited unless a Policy says otherwise.

### S.3.2 -- Tactical Meeting Process
Standard process:
1. Check-in Round
2. Checklist Review
3. Metrics Review
4. Progress Updates
5. Build Agenda
6. Triage Items
7. Closing Round

The Facilitator runs the process; the Secretary captures outputs. The Secretary is accountable for capturing and publishing Tactical Meeting outputs.

### S.3.3 -- Agenda Triage
The agenda item owner may make requests of another participant in their capacity as a Partner or representing a Role. Outputs captured by the Secretary include: actions committed, projects identified, and any tensions requiring governance.

---

## Article 4: Distributed Authority

### S.4.1 -- Role Authority
A Role-filler has authority to take any action or make any decision to enact the Role's Purpose or Accountabilities, as long as it doesn't break a rule in the Constitution.

### S.4.1.2 -- Domain Control
A Role-filler may impact Domains owned by their Role freely. To impact a Circle's Domains or another Role's Domains, they must request permission from the controlling Role.

### S.4.2.1 -- Interpretation Conflicts
When a Partner's interpretation of the Constitution and the Organization's Governance conflicts with another Partner's, the Secretary of the relevant Circle may rule on which interpretation to use and publish the ruling and the logic behind it for precedential guidance. Super-Circle Secretaries may override Circle Secretary rulings.

### S.4.2.2 -- Governance Validity
The Secretary may rule on the validity of any Governance and must strike it from the Circle's records if it violates constitutional rules. Any Circle Member may challenge governance validity by raising a tension with the Secretary.

## Article 5: Governance Process

### S.5.1 -- Governance Meeting Scheduling
The Secretary is accountable for scheduling regular Governance Meetings for the Circle, and must schedule special Governance Meetings promptly upon request of any Circle Member.

### S.5.1.2 -- Governance Records
The Secretary is accountable for capturing and publishing the outputs of the Circle's Governance Process and holds a Domain over the Circle's governance records.

### S.5.4 -- Governance Meeting Process
Standard process:
1. Check-in Round
2. Administrative concerns
3. Agenda building
4. Process each item (tension -> proposal -> clarifying questions -> reaction round -> objection round -> integration)
5. Closing Round

### S.5.5 -- Valid Objections
An objection is valid if it meets all four criteria:
1. The Proposal would reduce the Circle's capacity to express its Purpose or enact its Accountabilities
2. The Proposal would limit the Objector's authority to express their Role's Purpose or enact its Accountabilities
3. The Proposal creates this tension without the Objector's consent
4. The tension would exist even if the Proposer's tension were already resolved

### S.5.5.3 -- Objection Testing
The Facilitator tests objections against these criteria. The Secretary records valid objections and the integration outcomes that resolve them.

### S.5.3.6 -- Surrogate Secretary
When the Secretary is unavailable, the acting Facilitator assumes Secretary duties.

---

## Elected Role Terms (S.5.3)
The Secretary is elected via an Integrative Election Process. The term is either the Circle's default or whatever was specified at election. A new election must be called when the role becomes vacant.

## Key Interpretive Principles

**Tension vs. Preference**: A proposal must address a real tension felt by the proposer, not impose a "best practice" or preference on the circle. If no real tension exists for the proposer, the proposal is invalid.

**Objection validity threshold**: An objection must pass all four criteria. The Secretary (or Facilitator) tests each criterion sequentially. An objection that fails even one criterion is not valid.

**Constitution over local governance**: When local circle governance conflicts with the Constitution, the Constitution prevails. The Secretary strikes invalid governance from the record.

**Role authority vs. Circle authority**: A Role-filler has authority within their Role's Purpose and Accountabilities. They cannot make decisions that fall under another Role's domain without that Role's permission.

**Records as living documents**: Governance records reflect the current state of agreed governance. They are not static -- every governance meeting potentially changes them. GlassFrog is the authoritative runtime.
